Research Roadmap

Strategic milestones for EXOCORTEX Lab's development (2025–2027)

2025 Q3 – Research Review

Phase 1

Comprehensive analysis of existing literature, identification of research gaps, and establishment of experimental protocols.

Focus: Literature review, methodology development

2026 Q1 – Design Frameworks & Signal Mapping

Phase 2

Development of neural signal processing pipelines and initial interface prototypes for emotion recognition.

Focus: Algorithm design, signal processing

2026 Q4 – Robotic Prototypes (Simulated + Conceptual)

Phase 3

Creation of simulated robotic control systems and conceptual designs for emotion-adaptive interfaces.

Focus: Simulation, conceptual design

2027 Q2 – Open-Source Release + Pre-Startup Push

Phase 4

Release of research findings and algorithms as open-source, preparation for startup formation.

Focus: Knowledge sharing, community building
Note: Current research focuses exclusively on design frameworks, signal processing, and simulated prototypes. Manufacturing of physical systems is not part of the current roadmap.

About the Founder

Nabil Sajjad: Building the Neurotech Future

At 15, Nabil Sajjad is pioneering the next frontier of human-machine interaction. His journey into neurotechnology began with a simple question: "What if technology could understand us as deeply as we understand each other?"

Driven by a passion for neuroscience and robotics, Nabil founded EXOCORTEX Lab to develop ethical brain-computer interfaces that augment human capabilities without compromising our humanity. His work sits at the intersection of machine learning, affective computing, and neural engineering.

We're not just building technology—we're creating a new language for human-machine symbiosis. True innovation in BCI must respect cognitive liberty while expanding what humans can achieve.

EXOCORTEX Lab Philosophy

"Brains deserve machines that feel, not just obey." Our approach centers on creating technology that adapts to human emotional states, enhancing agency rather than replacing human decision-making.
